## Summary

The W101 is the highest-volume product in the entire group portfolio (#1 of 968, 4.27M units L12M, 10.7% of total sales). It sits at the intersection of nearly every customer conversation Westford Mill has: high-volume promo, retail bag-for-life, decorator merchandise, gifting, sustainability adjacency. That's why Luca called it the perfect test case — and why it deserves the most carefully designed PDP in the catalogue.

This brief covers the page section-by-section, with one explicit design call running through it: **the public PDP leans into design, decoration and colour breadth; logged-in trade view dials up stock, volume and commercial signal**. Both built on the same page, controlled by audience state.

---

## Objective

Translate the W101 product data, sales context and brand positioning into a populated PDP brief that (a) follows the proposed Westford Mill layout, (b) reflects Luca's commercial point about scale and availability, and (c) gives House Digital a clear, agency-ready content spec.

## Context (why this product, why now)

- **Group #1 by volume.** 4,272,379 units sold L12M. Bag for Life category sits 2nd by product type at 5.83M units across the W101 family alone. Largest single revenue driver in the business.
- **Family of three:** W101 (long handles, 48 colours), W101C (contrast handles, 5 colours), W101S (short handles, 5 colours). All same fabric, all 140gsm 100% cotton, Pakistan-made.
- **Wider Bag for Life family** within Westford Mill: 13 products including W801 EarthAware® Organic, W125 Maxi, W104 Mini, W103 Party, W107 Sling. The W101 is the gateway; the others extend the lane.
- **Decorator demand is the engine.** Classified Promo / Merchandise. The audience is print houses, decorators, distributors, agencies — and a smaller stream of independent retail/lifestyle buyers.
- **Luca's commercial point:** stock availability is itself a sales tool. The page should make that legible without making the brand feel cheap.

---

## Design calls baked into this brief

Six decisions worth flagging before the section-by-section detail:

1. **Decorated imagery leads the gallery, not natural flat-shots.** The W101 isn't bought to look at empty. Decorated examples carry the page; the natural shots support it.
2. **Pantone visible in the colour selector.** Standard requirement, but doubly important here — the 48-colour breadth is a primary sales argument. Each swatch needs its Pantone reference exposed (hover or persistent caption).
3. **Stock indicator is dynamic and prominent.** Per Luca's point — surfaces real availability. Logged-in trade view shows the full number ("501,128 in stock"). Public view shows a stock band ("In stock — high availability") to avoid feeling like a wholesale clearance.
4. **Two product-family blocks, not one.** A tight comparison ladder (W101 vs W101C vs W101S) AND a "More from the Bag for Life family" block (W801 organic, W125 maxi, W104 mini). The family is too rich to compress into a single row.
5. **Editorial slots at the bottom are real content, not decoration.** The two lifestyle blocks in the layout map to the blog/editorial linking Alex asked about. Brief calls these out as named editorial content with topics suggested.
6. **No Impact accordion row.** The W101 is not certified organic or recycled. Skip the impact stats block — that's W801 territory. The honesty here is chlorine-free bleaching (hydrogen peroxide), which goes in "What to expect", not in a fake impact frame.

## Brand voice — applied here

WM voice rules followed:

- **Material first** — lead with "100% cotton, 140gsm" not with adjectives
- **Considered, not bland** — every section earns its place; nothing is filler
- **Avoid Quadra drift** — no "engineered", "precision", "specification" language despite the technical decoration story
- **Avoid BagBase drift** — no "bold", "standout", "colour pop" despite the 48-colour story (used "considered" instead)
- **Discover** not Explore in CTAs (WM preference)
- **Canvas metaphor used sparingly** — "trusted canvas" appears once in the positioning blurb. Not overused. WM owns it but doesn't lean on it
- **Durability-sustainability bridge** referenced lightly via "made for daily use" and "lives in well over time" rather than overclaiming
- **No vague green claims** — chlorine-free bleaching is named specifically, not framed as "eco friendly"

## Dual-audience treatment (Luca's tension, resolved at the page level)

One page, two states. Same architecture, different emphasis.

| Element | Public view | Trade view (logged in) |
|---|---|---|
| Stock indicator | "High availability — ships from UK warehouse" | "501,128 in stock" (real figure) |
| Pricing | Hidden | Per-unit, MoQ, break points visible |
| USP Tile 3 (scale) | Optional — could swap to "Made for daily use" | Always shown — scale IS the message |
| Editorial slots | Retail/design-led editorial primary | Decorator/case-study editorial primary |
| CTAs | "Find a Distributor" prominent | "Find Stock" + "Access Assets" prominent |
| Comparison ladder | Same | Same |
| Decoration block | Same | Same — decoration is shared language |

**Why this works:** The page architecture doesn't change. The content variation is in 3–4 specific modules. Easier to build, easier to maintain, harder to mess up than two separate pages.

**What it does NOT solve:** Channel-specific messaging (Instagram, CRM, direct sales). That's downstream of the PDP and out of scope here. Worth a separate conversation off the back of this.
